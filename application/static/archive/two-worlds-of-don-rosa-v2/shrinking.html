Comments:

Before "The Incredible Shrinking Tightwad" appeared in America it went through some significant changes with some pages censored away and with an extra page added. Don Rosa explains that this way: I always intended to add one page of action to that story when Gladstone used it -- in those days I was strictly limited to a set number of pages (8+8+8=24) and could not add anything extra even if I wanted to. But *after* I'd added the new page, Gladstone found out that Disney wanted several panels censored from the story due to the references to hygienic matters, and dictated that the way the panels would be removed was by simply removing the entire pages on which they appeared! That story has only appeared in its full form in the German Rosa-album (though they screwed up the printing of the extra page and it looked awful), and in... uh... maybe it was the Greek KOMIX?
