King of the Klondike

King of the Klondike was not only the eighth episode of his "Life and Times of Scrooge McDuck" masterpiece, it was also the first of Don's stories to have a One and Three part version. Interestingly, with the exception of the text boxes in the European versions, The art for the three and single part versions is exactly the same, unlike all the other multi-part stories!

The Treasury of Croesus

This third iteration of multiple versions is a bit odd because the one part version never appeared in America, instead Americans got the three part, unlike usual. After examining the scans that follow, it may become obvious why Gladstone's editors printed the three part version. The army in the splash panel in the beginning of Part III looks much more daunting than its European counterpart, given only a wide panel. Like "King of the Klondike", part II of the 3 part version has the same art as the splash panel of the single part version, so we only posted one.

Hearts of the Yukon

Although not officially part of "The Life and Times of Scrooge McDuck", this story was published right after the 12+ part mini-series ended (for the first time). In this story Don's humor really shines, making it Tristan's favorite Don Rosa work. With a great story like this to kick of the Walt Disney Giant series, one wonders why it never made it through a years publication. Enough mindless banter, you want... the scans, right?

In this story, Scrooge and Donald encounter none other than, The Black Knight! Maybe something is lost in the re-translation, but looking the at the pair of scans we show here, the extra seems to appear in the one part version that wasn't in the 3-part splash panel, yet the splash panel seems to do a better job a explaing the situation. The Cowboy Captain of the Cutty Sark

Don's Latest story, "The Cowboy Captain of the Cutty Sark" is another iteration in the "Life and Times of Scrooge McDuck" mini-series. The most important fact about this story, is that as of yet it will be the last of Don's stories to be published in English, as at present Disney Comics are no longer published in America. Though this may be, we still expect to see diffrent versions of Rosa's work appearing in the rest of the world. For us in America: Lets keep our fingers crossed, and start learning Danish or German!
