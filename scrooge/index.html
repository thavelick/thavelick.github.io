Reasons I like Uncle Scrooge

Nostalgia

I started reading Uncle Scroogle comics at a time in my life whn I didn't have a lot of interests beyond computers and video games. Even though I don't read the comics often these days, they repersent a reminder in my life to keep my interests and horizons broad.

I like being contrarian

My interest in comics started around the time as my friends. But while they were getting into Superman and Spiderman I was Drawn to Disnesy comics. I got made fun of for reading comics for "kids", but I found the stories by Carl Barks and later others to have more depth and adventure than spiderman beating up a long line of baddies

Its a metophor for what capitalism could be and an interesting counterpoint to my socialist tendancies

Scrooge McDuck (unlike his Dickeansian counterpart) seems to have made his fortune without being too exploitive. He made his money from doing hard work

When I was young, I really liked computers: programming them, reading about them, breaking them, fixing them, playing video games, making proto-video games, playing with business target software I had no real use for, BBSs and more. If it was on a keyboard attached to a TV or monitor I loved it.

By the time I was 13, as my seen through my parent's eyes, was that I liked little else. My Legos were gathering dust. My toys were discarded and sold at garage sales. I'd occasionally read a book but not as veraciously as my parents did at my age, nor as much as my older sibling. My mom broke it to me: if I didn't come up with some additional things to do with my time I would face a terrible fate. I would grow up to become boring!

Taking this fear to heart, I set out to find a hobby. Sports? Even wheelchair basketball didn't hold any appeal. Stamp collecting? Why would I spend my money on something that's just going to sit on a shelf?
