Recently in an Effective Altruism chat room someone brought up question of whether or not rasing taxes on billionaires would be an effective way get more good done in the world. At first glance, this seems simple: Of course raising their taxes would lead to more money and resources for the needy. However, many billionaires give a lot of their money to charity. Some, like Bill Gates an Warren Buffet have pledge to give their entire fortunes to charity before they die. Thus, if many billionaires give away much of their fortunes and they so effectively to help a great number of people and if governments spend tax revenue inefficiently, or worse on things like building bombs,  such that money doesn't get into the hands of people and organizations that need it the most, then raising taxes on billionaires would actually do more harm than good! This article collects my thoughts and research on this question.

# Restrictions
To simplify analysis, I've restricted my research to the United States. Since billionaires live all over the world and their finances are increasingly global, further research into global policy is probably needed. But since I probably have little likelihood of affecting policy anywhere, I think the restriction is fair.

# Is U.S. Government spending effective?
Because this will likely prove to be a complex question it is probably useful to look at if there are any economic theories that can be brought to bare.

## Preliminary Conclusions
* Raising taxes arbitrarily likely wouldn't move the needle that much
* If policy was changed such that additional tax revenue went to redistribution, that probably *would* be effective
* What I would recommend:
  * Do away with the SS income cap (but keep the benefit cap)
  * Lower the ages for SS and Medicare benefits
  * Possibly support UBI
